Belur Math, established in 1897 on the west bank of the Hooghly River in Kolkata, India, is the headquarters of the Ramakrishna Math and Mission. Founded by Swami Vivekananda, it serves as a spiritual and educational center inspired by the teachings of Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa. The architectural marvel combines Hindu, Christian, and Islamic styles, embodying the harmony of religions.

Victoria Memorial, constructed in Kolkata, India, from 1906 to 1921, stands as a majestic tribute to Queen Victoria's memory. This grand marble edifice, blending Indo-Saracenic and British architectural styles, serves as a museum housing an extensive collection of artifacts from the British colonial era. It remains a symbol of Kolkata's rich history and cultural heritage, attracting visitors from around the world to marvel at its grandeur.

Howrah Bridge, an iconic cantilever bridge, was constructed over the Hooghly River in Kolkata, India, between 1936 and 1943. Originally named the New Howrah Bridge, it was later renamed Rabindra Setu after Rabindranath Tagore, the renowned poet and Nobel laureate. Connecting Kolkata with Howrah, the bridge serves as a vital transportation artery and symbolizes the city's architectural and engineering prowess.

Jorasanko Thakur Bari, located in Kolkata, India, was the ancestral home of the Tagore family, including Rabindranath Tagore, the Nobel laureate poet and writer. Built in the 18th century, it served as a center for Bengali cultural and intellectual activities during the Bengal Renaissance. Today, it stands as a museum preserving the legacy of the Tagore family and their contributions to literature and the arts.

Princep Ghat was built during the British colonial era in the mid-19th century and was named after James Prinsep, a British scholar and antiquary. The construction was completed in 1843. Initially, it served as a landing stage for loading and unloading goods from the Hooghly River.It evolved into a cultural landmark along Kolkata's Hooghly River. Witnessing historic events and political gatherings during the Indian independence movement, it remains a popular leisure spot. Recent renovations have preserved its heritage, offering scenic views and hosting cultural events, embodying Kolkata's rich history and architectural legacy.

Dakshineswar Kali Temple

Dakshineswar Kali Temple in Kolkata,a renowned Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kali, situated on the eastern bank of the Hooghly River. The temple was built by Rani Rashmoni, a philanthropist and a devotee of Kali, in 1855. The temple complex is vast and includes the main temple dedicated to Kali,One of its notable features is the twelve identical shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva, known as the "Baro Shiva." The temple gained widespread recognition due to the association with Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, a mystic and saint who served as its priest in the 19th century. Today, Dakshineswar Kali Temple attracts a large number of devotees and tourists who come to pay their respects and witness its architectural beauty and spiritual ambiance.

Alipore Zoological Garden

Alipore Zoo, established in 1876 in Kolkata, India, is the country's oldest zoological park. Inaugurated by Edward VII, then Prince of Wales,and designed by Carl Louis Schwendler, a German architect. It spans 46.5 acres and has hosted numerous notable animals, including the giant tortoise Adwaita, who lived over 250 years until 2006.Over the years, Alipore Zoo has evolved into a significant center for wildlife conservation, education, and research. It houses a diverse collection of animals, including rare and endangered species, within its sprawling grounds.The zoo's mission extends beyond entertainment, focusing on the conservation of biodiversity and the promotion of environmental awareness among visitors.

Eden Gardens Stadium

Eden Gardens, situated in Kolkata, India, is one of the most iconic cricket stadiums in the world. Established in 1864, it holds the distinction of being one of the oldest and largest cricket grounds in India.Named after the sister of Lord Auckland, the then Governor-General of India, the stadium has witnessed numerous historic cricketing moments over the years. It became the home venue for the Bengal cricket team and the Kolkata Knight Riders franchise in the Indian Premier League (IPL). Eden Gardens has a rich legacy, having hosted several memorable matches, including the 1987 World Cup final and the famous 2001 Test match between India and Australia, dubbed the 'Greatest Test Match Ever' by Wisden.With a seating capacity of over 66,000 spectators, Eden Gardens is renowned for its electrifying atmosphere during cricket matches, making it a favorite destination for players and fans alike.

Marble Palace

The Marble Palace, located in Kolkata, India, is an iconic landmark renowned for its opulent architecture and extensive art collection. Built in the mid-19th century by Raja Rajendra Mullick, a wealthy Bengali merchant with a penchant for European aesthetics, the palace stands as a testament to the grandeur of the bygone era.Constructed entirely of marble imported from Italy and featuring intricate sculptures and ornate embellishments, the Marble Palace exudes elegance and luxury. Its interiors are adorned with priceless works of art, including paintings by renowned artists such as Rubens, Reynolds, and Titian, as well as rare antiques, sculptures, and artifacts from around the world.The palace has played host to illustrious guests, including royalty, dignitaries, and luminaries from various fields. It has also served as a cultural hub, hosting extravagant gatherings, concerts, and art exhibitions over the years.
